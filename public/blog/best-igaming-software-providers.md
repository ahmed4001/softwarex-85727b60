## What "iGaming software provider" actually means

The category splits into three layers:

1. **Platform / iGaming OS** — player accounts, wallet, bonus engine, CRM, KYC, anti-fraud, regulatory reporting.
2. **Game studios / content** — slots, live dealer, table games, crash games. Plugged in via aggregator APIs.
3. **Sportsbook engine** — odds feed, risk management, in-play trading, cash-out.

A "full-stack" provider sells all three; many operators mix-and-match (platform + 30–50 aggregated studios + a sportsbook overlay).

The "best" provider depends on your target market. A Curaçao-licensed crypto casino has very different needs than a New Jersey iCasino:

- **LATAM (Brazil 2025 framework):** SoftSwiss, Salsa Technology, Pragmatic Play
- **US (state-by-state):** GAN, IGT PlayDigital, Light & Wonder, Kambi

## Pricing models you'll see

- **Setup fee:** $20k–$250k depending on customization
- **Monthly platform fee:** $5k–$30k
- **Revenue share:** 5–15% of GGR (platform) + 10–20% of GGR (aggregated games)
- **White-label all-in:** typically 40–60% of NGR

Always model 12-month TCO, not month-one fees — bonus engine, payments, and KYC vendor passthrough costs are where the real spend sits.

## Common procurement mistakes

1. **Underestimating compliance overhead.** A change of jurisdiction = months of recertification.
2. **Locking into a single content provider.** Aggregator APIs exist for a reason — diversify.
3. **Ignoring data ownership.** Make sure your contract gives you player-level event data in raw form.
4. **Buying sportsbook + casino from one vendor when you only need one well.** Best-of-breed beats convenience.
